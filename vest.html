SuperVest: Compression Vest for Autism Spectrum Disorders

Problem Statement

Children diagnosed with Level 3 Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) are at higher risk of becoming emotionally dysregulated when switching between activities, or when exposed to overstimulating environments. These moments can be difficult to detect, pre-empt, and manage, and are equally challenging for caregivers who might intervene to interrupt the behavior transition.

Hypothesis

Theory of Change

There is room for intervention for a garment that can respond to an autistic child’s emotions immediately by providing comfort and decreasing anxiety.

Theory of Action

If young children with autism receive comfort in the form of pressure, they will feel more instantaneous relief during an “autistic meltdown” so that they are more confident during these episodes in the future.

Students in Early Education with Level 3 Autism Spectrum Disorders

Moodboard & Personas

Minimum Viable Product Specifications

  1. Track biofeedback (automatic, learns behaviors)
    • Capable of passively monitoring Electrodermal Activity (EDA)
    • Capable of passively monitoring Heart Rate
    • Capable of processing emotional arousal and valence, eliminating noisy data generated while performing strenuous activity/exercise
  2. Subtle design
    • Blends in with other clothing
    • Does not loudly inflate/deflate–the wearer should not be socially punished by the garment
  3. Retreat button (manual)
    • The user must be able to manually control (stop/start) actuation
  4. Comfortable
    • Appropriate fabric
    • Straps, bands, etc do not create unnecessary strain or discomfort on the wearer
  5. Compression
    • Compression is administered automatically, applying a reasonable level of pressure to the upper body in response to changes in emotional arousal (stress, anxiety, etc as inferred from sensor data and ML signal processing
    • The garment decompresses automatically so that future applications of pressure may be felt by the wearer
